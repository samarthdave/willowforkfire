Engine 81

A 2012 Pierce Pumper equipped with a 1500 gallon per minute fire pump and a compressed air foam system which allows us to inject several types of firefighting foam into the hose lines depending upon the type of material burning. This apparatus is equipped with a full complement of hand and power tools which allows firefighters to gain access to fires and persons in need by whatever means necessary, as well as medical equipment for EMS responses. This truck is also equipped with an onboard generator allowing us to run lights and various power tools in areas which lack power.

Booster 81

A 2014 Ford F550 4-wheel drive vehicle with a firefighting skid mounted to the back. This skid includes a 300-gallon water tank which supplies a gas power pump capable of flowing 150 gallons per minute. This vehicle is set up for off road grass and brush fires carrying a 2 - 100’ hose reels, spray nozzles attached to the front bumper for spraying while driving down a fire line. The truck carries hand tools used in gaining access through fences and gates, a chain saw for cutting trees and heavy brush and a complement of fire extinguishers. This truck is equipped with a winch utilized in moving heavy objects as well as assisting the truck when stuck in mud or sand.

Tower 81

A 2010 Pierce 100’ elevating platform with a 2000 gallon per minute fire pump. This apparatus has the capability of directing 2000 gallons a minute from 2 nozzles located in the platform on the tip of the ladder. We can also supply handlines from the apparatus allowing firefighters to perform firefighting operations from the ground level. Besides being able to provide an elevated fire attack, this truck is set up for rescue, ventilation and salvage. There is a full complement of hand and power tools to assist in these operations as well as heavy hydraulic cutters, rams and spreaders used in vehicle rescue or to reach people trapped by heavy objects or other obstructions as well as medical equipment used in EMS responses. By utilizing the tower, ropes and baskets carried on this truck we can safely rescue people from higher elevations as well as below grade. This apparatus carries an onboard generator to power onboard lighting sufficient to illuminate scenes at night as well as run needed power tools.

Tanker 82

A 2005 U.S. Tanker built fire apparatus with a 2000-gallon water tank and 500 gallon per minute pump. While this apparatus can stage an initial fire attack with hose carried, its primary purpose is to supply other fire apparatus with water in areas that do not have fire hydrants. This truck is equipped with a folding water tank capable of holding the 2000 gallons carried and can be rapidly filled using dump valves which will empty the contents in 1 minute or less. This allows the truck to locate a water source and refill thus shuttling water from the water source to the fire ground.

Battalion 80

A 2016 Ford Expedition used by the on-duty Battalion Chief. This vehicle is used for incident command on large scale incidents and is equipped with an incident command console containing communication equipment and an accountability system which allows the Battalion Chief to track the status of all firefighters on the scene electronically through devices located on their protective clothing.
